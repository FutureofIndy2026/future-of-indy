Meet Patrick McAuley

Patrick is a commercial real estate professional and lifelong Hoosier running for Congress in Indiana’s 7th Congressional District. In January 2025, he started Future of Indy to listen first and build an agenda based on what voters in Indianapolis say matters most.

Patrick works with small businesses, property owners, and neighborhoods across Marion County. That experience has shown him both the opportunities in our city and the red tape and safety concerns that hold people back.

Through Future of Indy coffee chats, roundtables, and one-on-one conversations, Patrick is gathering ideas from residents, faith leaders, first responders, and community organizations in every corner of the district.

His promise is simple: listen, be honest, and put Indianapolis ahead of party politics.

Four Priorities for the Future of Indy

These are the core issues Patrick hears about again and again in Indiana’s 7th District.

Public Safety & Trust
Public Safety
  • Support police, firefighters, and first responders with training and staffing.
  • Encourage community policing and transparency that rebuilds trust.
  • Help officers, teachers, and nurses live in the neighborhoods they serve.
Workforce Housing
Housing
  • Promote attainable housing for working families close to jobs and transit.
  • Cut red tape that slows down responsible, community-led projects.
  • Reward cities that streamline permitting and focus on workforce housing.
Apprenticeships & Skilled Trades
Apprenticeships
  • Strengthen connections between high schools, career centers, unions, and employers.
  • Grow apprenticeships in construction, healthcare, logistics, tech, and manufacturing.
  • Highlight opportunities in the trades for women and under-represented groups.
Homelessness with Accountability & Hope
Homelessness
  • Partner with nonprofits, churches, and charities focused on recovery and mental health.
  • Support transitional housing tied to treatment, job training, and responsibility.
  • Keep downtown, parks, and business areas safe, clean, and open for everyone.
