Why Future of Indy?

Patrick launched Future of Indy in January 2025 to ask a simple question: What do people in Indianapolis care about most?

  • Safe neighborhoods and trusted police–community relationships
  • Workforce housing close to jobs and schools
  • Real apprenticeships and trade pathways
  • A smarter, compassionate approach to homelessness

Meet Patrick McAuley

Patrick is a commercial real estate professional, lifelong Hoosier, and community leader running for Congress in Indiana’s 7th District. He believes Indianapolis deserves safer streets, attainable housing, and opportunity for every neighborhood.

Local roots

Indianapolis first.

Every day, Patrick works with small businesses, property owners, and neighborhood leaders across Marion County. His experience in commercial real estate gives him a close look at both Indy’s potential and the barriers that hold it back.

In early 2025, Patrick launched Future of Indy as a grassroots effort to listen — hosting coffees, roundtables, and conversations with residents, business owners, faith leaders, and first responders.

Patrick’s commitment is simple: listen first, lead with common sense, and put Indianapolis ahead of Washington politics.

Patrick’s Priorities

Four pillars guiding Patrick’s vision for Indianapolis.

Public Safety & Trust

Support law enforcement, strengthen community policing, and rebuild trust.

Workforce Housing

Cut red tape and encourage attainable housing near job centers and transit.

Apprenticeships & Trades

Expand skilled-trade pathways and partner with employers, unions, and schools.

Homelessness Solutions

A compassionate but accountable response focused on recovery and stability.

What is Future of Indy?

A district-wide listening initiative launched January 2025 to hear directly from residents about public safety, housing, jobs, and homelessness — and to shape real solutions rooted in community feedback.
